Getting Started

Introduction

Minimob’s dashboard for Advertising is a self-service tool that enables advertisers to create and manage their campaigns on their own. Through the dashboard, Advertisers can quickly and effortlessly set up campaigns, monitor their performance and manage the relevant budgets.

Apps

Start by creating an App

Adding an App can be as easy as just entering the app’s store URL, and the details of the app will be automatically retrieved. But there is also the option to specify the details by hand. Media assets are uploaded and stored for each app and they can be shared across all campaigns of the same app. Suspending all the campaigns of an app takes only one click, for setting the particular app as Inactive.

Campaigns

… then create Campaigns

Multiple campaigns can lie under the same app (however, a campaign can belong only to a single app). Numerous parameters are available for modelling the characteristics of a campaign (e.g. bidding, budgeting, targeting, etc.). The campaign’s media assets can be picked from the app’s existing collection or new ones can be directly uploaded. Each campaign can be individually set to run as scheduled or forced to stop.

Billing

Check and replenish account’s Balance

Campaigns are funded by the credit that is available, thus the current balance should be sufficient for running the active campaigns. Refilling the account can be done through PayPal or Wire transfer, while bonus credits can be rewarded in the context of Minimob’s customer loyalty programs.

Reports

View Reports

Performance and monetary information for campaigns and apps (such as clicks, conversions, conversion rates and amount spent) is reported. The way that results are grouped is configurable while it is possible to zoom in and view more detailed information for each group. The reported metrics can be filtered while their evolution over time is graphically presented in comprehensive charts.

Quick guides

Apps

Apps List

At the Apps menu, you can create a new app (by clicking New App) as well as see and manage the apps that you have already created.

The list displays all the created apps along with information about each one. Clicking on the name of an app opens a new tab where you can view and edit the details of the particular app. You can have multiple tabs open at the same time, but if you navigate away from the Apps menu, they will all be closed.

The number of Clicks and Conversions as well as the Cost for each app across all its campaigns are presented, along with the number of campaigns that are currently active.

Under Status, you can see or select whether an app is Active or not. If you make an app Inactive then all associated campaigns are paused, i.e. are not marketed through Minimob.

App Overview

App creation

To create a new app, you need to specify its name and then click Create. The default state of the new app is Inactive. You can activate the app later on, after you have completed setting up the details of the app.

The information that is displayed for the app include:

  • Package Name: the unique ID that identifies the application (e.g. com.developer.appname or id123456789)
  • Store url: The URL through which the app is available from the relevant app store
  • Store: The name of the app store through which the app is marketed
  • OS Platform: The operating system of the app
  • App price: The price tag of the app (a value of ‘0’ means the app is free)
  • Category: The category under which the app is listed at the app store
  • Version: The version of the app that is currently available
  • Updated: The date of the most recent update of the app
  • Developer: The name of the person/company who develops the app
  • App Description: The description of the app (typically, the description of the app that is displayed at the app store)

After the app has been created you can proceed with adding campaigns and media assets while you can also modify the app’s details. Any modifications you make are automatically saved upon moving your cursor away from the modified field.

To change the app icon, you can browse and select a new image file or drag and drop it on the image placeholder.

App creation by Import

You can specify the app’s details by hand or you can do that automatically by simply entering the app’s Store URL and then clicking Import. The details of the app are retrieved from the app store and are automatically imported. You may want to review the imported information and make any modifications, if necessary. Once done, click Create to create the new app.

App campaigns

Once the app has been created you can proceed with adding campaigns and media assets.

To add a new campaign, click New Campaign. A new tab is displayed where you can specify the campaign’s details. The new campaign is automatically associated with the particular app and after its creation it is added to the campaigns list of the app.

Under Status, you can see or select whether a campaign of the particular app is Active or not. If you make a campaign Inactive then it is no longer marketed through Minimob.

The Ready indicator shows whether a campaign is currently running or not. In case a campaign is not running, placing the mouse over the indicator lists the reason(s) why the campaign is not running.

App media assets

To upload a new media asset, click New Media asset. A pop-up is displayed where you can browse for an image file or drag and drop it on the image placeholder. The new media asset is automatically associated with the particular app and it is added to the media assets list of the app.

Clicking the Configure button next to a media asset of the app displays a page for configuring its properties. The properties of a media asset can also be configured when editing a campaign. Note that no matter whether the configuration of a media asset was initiated from within an app or any of the app’s underlying campaigns, the properties defined for that media asset are the same across the app and all of its campaigns.

In the case of configuring a video media asset, you have the option to specify an End-card (i.e. an image that is shown after the video has finished). End-card images can be selected from images already uploaded for the app, or new images can be uploaded. New images are promptly assigned to the video media asset while by default they are allowed to be used only in the context of video ads. However, it is possible to change this by configuring the properties of the particular image media asset and specifying that it can be used for any ad.

Start or Stop App

Once you have completed setting up the details of the app, you can activate it by clicking on the Start/Stop app. All campaigns of an app that has been stopped are not marketed through Minimob.

Campaigns

Campaigns List

At the Campaigns menu, you can create a new campaign (by clicking New Campaign) as well as see and manage the campaigns that you have already created.

The list displays all the created campaigns along with information about each one. Clicking on the name of a campaign opens a new tab where you can view or edit the details of the particular campaign. In addition, clicking on the name of the app (to which the campaign belongs) opens a new tab where you can view or edit the details of the app. You can have multiple tabs open at the same time, but if you navigate away from the Campaigns menu, they will all be closed.

The number of the current Clicks and Conversions as well as the Cost are presented for each campaign, along with its Creation, Start and End dates. For each campaign, you can also view targeting, bidding and budgeting information (such as: Target Countries, Traffic Type, Payout Model and Bid, Conversion and Budget Caps, etc.).

Under Status, you can see or select whether a campaign is Active or not. If you make a campaign Inactive then it is no longer marketed through Minimob.

The Ready indicator shows whether the campaign is currently running or not. In case the campaign is not running, placing the mouse over the indicator lists the reason(s) why the campaign is not running.

Overview

Campaign creation

To create a new campaign, you need to specify its name as well as the other required details, and then click Create. Note that you cannot change the app of the campaign after the creation of the campaign. The default state of the new campaign is Inactive; you can activate it later on, after you have completed setting up the details of the campaign.

Once the campaign has been created you can proceed with adding media assets while you can also modify the campaign’s details. Any modifications you make are automatically saved upon moving your cursor away from the modified field.

Note that the campaign cannot run until mandatory details are specified and unless certain conditions are satisfied. In such cases, the relevant notifications are displayed. Clicking on a notification directs you to the exact point where your input or action is required.

Schedule

Optionally, you can set the Start and End date of the campaign. Click on the corresponding calendar icon to select a date. While at the calendar view, you may click on the watch icon to specify the time as well. If you do not specify the time, the current time is used.

If you do not specify a Start date, the campaign starts immediately after being activated. If you do not specify an End date, the campaign will continue to run as long as it is Active.

Targeting

The values for OS Platform and App Store are always inherited from the app and cannot be changed.

The Traffic Type can be only Incentivized, only Non-Incentivized, both or neither.

Click on Target Countries to search by name and select one or more countries to target.

Bidding and Budgeting

Click on Model and select one of the supported pricing models, i.e. CPI (Cost-Per-Install) or CPA (Cost-Per-Action). Then, specify the Bid price; the currency of the bid price is always in USD.

In order to be allowed to define Caps, you must have first specified a Bid price. Once so, you can select either Conversions or Budget to set Conversion or Budget caps respectively. If you select Conversions then Budget caps are automatically calculated and vice versa; if you specify Budget caps, the calculated values for the conversions are rounded to the nearest integer.

Tracking

The Minimob platform employs postback tracking (a.k.a. “server-side tracking”) for keeping track of conversions. When serving a campaign through our affiliate ad networks, the Click URL contains parameters that identify the campaign and each individual click action for the particular campaign, while it can also carry information about the ad network.

This information should be stored on your side and, upon a conversion, fired back to Minimob via the Postback URL, in order to correlate the specific conversion with the corresponding user transaction that was achieved through the respective ad network.

In this way, each conversion can be properly tracked and credited to the corresponding marketing partner, while it also helps to understand the analytics of the campaigns and thus to improve its performance.

Click the Edit button to view instructions about generating and integrating the relevant Click URL and Postback URL for this campaign.

Media assets

To add a new media asset to the campaign, click New Media asset. A pop-up is displayed where you can:

  • Select a media asset that is already uploaded to the relevant app: At the App Media assets list, click the switch under the Used in this Campaign column to add or remove the corresponding media asset from the particular campaign. Note: Deleting a media asset from the App Media assets list deletes it from the app (and all its underlying campaigns).
  • Upload a new media asset: browse for an image file or drag and drop it on the image placeholder. The new media asset is automatically added to the corresponding app and it is made Active for the particular campaign.

Clicking the Configure button next to a media asset of the campaign displays a page for configuring its properties. The properties of a media asset can also be configured when editing the app where the campaign belongs to. Note that no matter whether the configuration of a media asset was initiated from within an app or any of the app’s underlying campaigns, the properties defined for that media asset are the same across the app and all of its campaigns.

In the case of configuring a video media asset, you have the option to specify an End-card (i.e. an image that is shown after the video has finished). End-card images can be selected from images already uploaded for the app, or new images can be uploaded. New images are promptly assigned to the video media asset while by default they are allowed to be used only in the context of video ads. However, it is possible to change this by configuring the properties of the particular image media asset and specifying that it can be used for any ad.

Tip: It is advised not to mix images and videos under the same campaign. If you want to use videos, you can create a separate campaign whose media assets are only videos. In this way you can use different settings (e.g. payouts) for video campaigns.
Start or Stop Campaign

Once you have completed setting up the details of the campaign, you can activate it by clicking on the Start/Stop campaign. A campaign that has been stopped is not marketed through Minimob.

Billing

At the Billing menu, you can view comprehensive information about your current balance and past payments, as well as replenish your account.

Your campaigns are funded by the credit that is available at your account. Thus, please make sure that you have sufficient balance, so that your campaigns can be marketed through the Minimob platform.

You can refill your account by making payments through PayPal or Wire Transfer. Simply specify the amount, select the payment method and then follow the relevant instructions. Once the payment is successfully completed, the corresponding amount is added to your balance.

In addition, you may receive a Bonus with regard to one of your previous payments (stated at the Referral Payment ID) or be rewarded with extra Credits, as part of the Minimob’s customer loyalty programs.

Under Payment History, you can view information about your payments and their status. Clicking on the Preview icon displays details about the particular payment.
